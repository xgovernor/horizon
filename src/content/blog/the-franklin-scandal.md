Welcome to an extended entry in our private knowledge library. In late 1988, the failure of the Franklin Community Federal Credit Union in Omaha, Nebraska, triggered investigations that quickly expanded beyond financial fraud into explosive allegations of a child sex trafficking and abuse network. Lawrence E. "Larry" King Jr., the credit union's manager and a prominent local Republican figure, was at the center. Alleged victims claimed that children from foster care, Boys Town, and other vulnerable backgrounds were sexually abused at parties, flown across the country for exploitation by powerful men, and subjected to drugs, pornography, and ritualistic elements. Names of prominent Nebraska businessmen, politicians, law enforcement officials, and even national figures were whispered as participants. A special Nebraska legislative committee investigated, hiring private investigator Gary Caradori, whose work produced videotaped victim statements. However, two grand juries (state and federal) in 1990 concluded the sex abuse claims were unfounded—a "carefully crafted hoax"—and some accusers faced perjury charges. King was convicted of financial crimes, serving 15 years. Books by former state senator John DeCamp (*The Franklin Cover-Up*, 1992) and journalist Nick Bryant (*The Franklin Scandal*, 2009) allege a massive cover-up protecting elites, while official records emphasize the lack of corroborating evidence for the abuse ring. The case remains highly controversial, with proponents seeing institutional protection of pedophile networks and skeptics viewing it as a mix of real financial crime with fabricated or unreliable abuse stories. In this document, we examine the financial collapse, abuse allegations and victim claims, key investigations and grand jury findings, legal outcomes, books alleging cover-up, and the enduring debates—all drawn from court records, grand jury reports, declassified or public investigative materials, contemporary news, and the primary texts on both sides.

## Background and the Franklin Credit Union Collapse

The Franklin Community Federal Credit Union was founded to serve Omaha's poor, particularly the Black community on the city's north side. Larry King, a charismatic Republican activist and singer who performed at national events, rose to become its manager and treasurer. By the mid-1980s, the institution appeared successful, with King enjoying a lavish lifestyle, private jets, and connections to powerful figures. In November 1988, federal regulators raided the credit union after irregularities surfaced. Investigations revealed massive embezzlement: the credit union had secretly issued millions in certificates of deposit that were not properly recorded, leading to a shortfall estimated at around $38–40 million. The National Credit Union Administration took over, and the institution collapsed. King's financial crimes became the initial focus, but rumors quickly spread of far darker activities tied to the missing funds—allegations that King used the credit union as a front for a child prostitution ring, with proceeds funding parties and transportation of minors for sexual exploitation by elites.

**Trust note**: The financial embezzlement is undisputed. King was indicted on multiple counts and pled guilty in 1991 to assisting in the embezzlement of approximately $39 million, receiving a 15-year sentence. This core fraud is documented in federal court records and National Credit Union Administration reports.

# Franklin Credit Union Key Financial Facts

- Manager: Lawrence E. "Larry" King Jr.
- Collapse: November 1988 federal raid
- Embezzlement: ~$38–40 million in unrecorded certificates of deposit
- Conviction: King pled guilty in 1991 to financial crimes; sentenced to 15 years


## The Abuse Allegations and Victim Testimonies

As the financial probe widened, social service workers and others reported claims of child sexual abuse linked to King. Alleged victims, many former foster children or wards of the state, described being taken to parties at locations like the French Cafe or private apartments (e.g., Twin Towers in Omaha), where they were abused by groups of men, given drugs, and sometimes transported by plane to other cities (including Washington, D.C.). Specific claims included:
- Sexual abuse, group sex, and pornography involving minors.
- Involvement of prominent local figures (businessmen, police, media personalities, clergy) and some national politicians.
- Ritualistic or satanic elements in some accounts (e.g., animal mutilation or worse, though these were less central).
- Use of Boys Town orphanage residents in some allegations.

Key alleged victims whose testimonies gained attention included Alisha Owen, Troy Boner, Danny King, and Paul Bonacci. Private investigator Gary Caradori, hired by the Nebraska Legislature's special Franklin committee, recorded videotaped interviews with some of these individuals in 1989–1990. The accounts were detailed and, in some cases, consistent across witnesses interviewed separately. Caradori died in a plane crash in July 1990 while returning from a lead-gathering trip; his death was ruled accidental but fueled suspicion among believers in a cover-up.

**Trust note on testimonies**: The initial videotaped statements provided graphic details and named specific individuals and locations. However, several witnesses (including Boner and Danny King) later recanted before grand juries, claiming the stories were fabricated or coached. Alisha Owen and Paul Bonacci largely stood by their claims. Credibility issues include inconsistencies, prior criminal records or vulnerabilities of some witnesses, and lack of physical corroboration (no photos, videos, or forensic evidence of the alleged abuse ring surfaced publicly).

## Investigations and Grand Jury Findings

Multiple probes occurred:
- Federal authorities (FBI, U.S. Attorney) and Nebraska state investigators examined both the financial crimes and abuse claims.
- A special Nebraska Legislature committee (with Senator Ernie Chambers prominent) conducted its own inquiry.
- **Douglas County Grand Jury (1990)**: After months of review, concluded the sex abuse allegations were a "carefully crafted hoax," scripted by someone familiar with Omaha institutions. It indicted Alisha Owen on perjury counts.
- **Federal Grand Jury (1990)**: Similarly found the abuse claims unfounded; indicted Owen on additional perjury charges and pursued King's financial case.

Owen was convicted of perjury in 1991 and served about 4.5 years. She maintained her allegations were true. No charges related to child sex abuse or trafficking were ever filed against King or the named prominent figures. The grand juries criticized the handling of witness statements and found insufficient credible evidence to support the ring.

**Trust note**: The grand jury reports are primary official records. They explicitly rejected the organized abuse claims as a hoax. Critics (including DeCamp and Bryant) argue the juries were influenced, witnesses intimidated (especially by FBI interviews), and exculpatory evidence ignored. No independent forensic validation of the abuse claims has been publicly confirmed.

## Legal Outcomes and King's Conviction

- Larry King was convicted solely on financial embezzlement/fraud charges, not abuse. He served time and later maintained the sex allegations were "garbage."
- Alisha Owen's perjury conviction stood (with some appeals); she has continued to assert the truth of her experiences in later years.
- No prominent named figures faced charges related to the alleged sex ring.
- Civil suits by some alleged victims (e.g., Paul Bonacci) resulted in default judgments or small awards in some instances due to non-appearance by defendants, but these were not criminal findings of guilt.

## Books Alleging Cover-Up and Alternative Narratives

- **John DeCamp – *The Franklin Cover-Up* (1992)**: Former Nebraska state senator and attorney for some victims. Claims a nationwide pedophile network protected by elites, involving drugs, money laundering (possibly tied to Iran-Contra), satanism, and murders. Alleges Caradori's plane crash was suspicious and details witness intimidation.
- **Nick Bryant – *The Franklin Scandal* (2009)**: Investigative journalist who spent years on the case. Argues for the reality of the trafficking ring, using victim interviews, documents, and inconsistencies in official probes. Extends claims to Washington, D.C., and broader elite networks.

These works compile witness statements, legislative memos (including DeCamp's own 1990 memo naming names), and argue that financial investigations were used to distract from or bury the abuse probe. They portray the grand juries as whitewashes.

**Trust note on books**: DeCamp and Bryant provide extensive documentation and interviews not always in official records. However, they rely heavily on witness credibility that grand juries rejected. Mainstream sources (NYT, Wikipedia summaries drawing on court records) emphasize the hoax findings and lack of corroboration.

## Conclusion

The Franklin Scandal originated with the 1988 collapse of the Franklin Community Federal Credit Union in Omaha and the embezzlement conviction of its manager, Larry King. It exploded into allegations of a child sex trafficking ring involving vulnerable minors exploited at parties by prominent local and possibly national figures. Victim testimonies described abuse, drugs, and interstate transportation, but two grand juries in 1990 ruled the sex abuse claims a "carefully crafted hoax," leading to perjury convictions for some accusers like Alisha Owen. No charges for organized child abuse or trafficking were ever brought against King or others named. Books by John DeCamp and Nick Bryant argue vigorously for a massive cover-up protecting elites, citing witness consistency, investigator obstacles, and institutional failures. Official records emphasize the lack of corroborating evidence beyond the financial crimes. The case illustrates how allegations of elite child exploitation can emerge from real institutional scandals, only to face fierce official rejection, leaving deep divisions over witness credibility, investigative thoroughness, and potential protection of the powerful.
